🐹 Mole Tap! — How to Play & Strategy Guide

From basic rules to high-score strategies · Updated 2026.07

Basic Rules

Mole Tap! is a reaction game: moles pop out of a 3×3 grid of holes, and you tap them with a squeaky toy hammer before they disappear. Classic mode gives you 30 seconds to score as much as possible; Survival mode has no timer — you play until you lose all 3 lives. No install, no sign-up: it runs right in your browser.

Combo & Multiplier

Consecutive hits build your combo. Every 5 combo raises your score multiplier one step, up to ×4 — a regular mole worth 10 points becomes worth 40. Missing a mole, tapping an empty hole, or hitting a bomb resets the combo to zero. The real key to a high score isn't tapping faster; it's keeping the chain alive.

Fever Time (Classic only)

In Classic mode, every time your combo reaches a multiple of 10, Fever Time kicks in for 5 seconds: moles spawn twice as fast, all points are doubled, and no bombs appear — so you can tap freely. Survival mode has no Fever; it's a pure endurance contest.

Boss Strategy

In Classic, a boss appears when 15 seconds remain; in Survival, one appears every 30 kills. A boss stays for 6 seconds and takes 5 hits to defeat. Don't tunnel-vision on the boss — regular moles you miss in the meantime will break your combo (or cost lives in Survival). The +100 defeat bonus scales with your multiplier, so killing a boss during Fever is the biggest jackpot in the game.

7 Tips for a High Score

  1. Use two thumbs. Splitting the screen between both hands doubles your coverage.
  2. Look at the center. Don't chase holes with your eyes — watch the middle and let peripheral vision detect movement.
  3. Name the bomb. Mentally saying "bomb!" when you see one suppresses the reflex to tap it.
  4. Golden moles first. Hesitate half a second and they're gone.
  5. Helmets: same spot, twice. Keep your finger there and tap again in rhythm.
  6. Play safe near Fever. At combo 8–9, don't gamble on a risky mole.
  7. Practice on Easy. Learn to keep combos alive on Easy before moving up.
